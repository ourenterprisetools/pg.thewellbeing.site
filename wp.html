10 Foods That Help Support Healthy Blood-Sugar Levels

The following foods are rich in fibre, healthy fats and antioxidants â€” nutrients that may assist in maintaining balanced glucose when part of an overall healthy lifestyle.

Oats

The beta-glucan fibre in oats slows digestion, leading to a steadier rise in post-meal glucose.

Beans & Legumes

Protein-rich and low on the glycaemic index, beans can promote satiety and more stable blood sugar.

Dark Leafy Greens

Spinach, kale and similar greens supply magnesium and antioxidants that support insulin function.

Fatty Fish

Salmon, sardines and tuna deliver omega-3 fats that may reduce inflammation and benefit heart health.

Berries

Berries combine fibre with polyphenols, offering a sweet option with a modest glycaemic impact.

Almonds & Other Nuts

Healthy fats, protein and magnesium in nuts can help curb post-meal sugar spikes.

Sweet Potatoes

They provide slow-digesting carbs and beta-carotene, making them a lower-GI alternative to regular potatoes.

Greek Yogurt

Plain Greek yogurt offers protein and probiotics that can aid fullness and gut health.

Extra-Virgin Olive Oil

Rich in monounsaturated fats, EVOO may improve lipid profiles when used in place of saturated fat.

Avocado

Avocados supply fibre and heart-healthy fats that slow digestion and support balanced blood sugar.
